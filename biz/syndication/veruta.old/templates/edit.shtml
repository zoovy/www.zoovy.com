Minimum Qualifying Events:

REMINDER: You can use Visitor Tagging to control your marketing spend. Once a visitor is tagged you will be responsible for marketing charges incurred by Veruta. Minimum Qualifying Events is the counter for how many events (ex: "product views", "cart views") a visitor must perform before they will be tagged. Tagging Probability will then determine if the visitor should be tagged. For example if tag products, and tag cart are both enabled, with a minimum of 3 qualifying events, and a probability of 1:3 - then only 1 in 3 people who view either three products, or two products and the cart page would be tagged. A "1" qualifying event, and 1:1 probability will cause all buyers to be tagged and result in the highest marketing spend.

Suppression Events (**OPTIONAL**)
Supression events cause the session to stop being remarketed to. Please keep in mind that if Google Checkout or Checkout By Amazon are in use then it is not possible to supress checkout for those visitors.
