e-commerce platforms

Zoovy Inc continues to be profitable, celebrating it's best quarter ever in Q4 2008, however the same cannot be said for many over-financed 'giants' in the e-commerce provider industry


September 2008 - ChannelAdvisor executives among 70 laid off

A recent round of layoffs at Morrisville’s ChannelAdvisor didn’t hit only the company’s rank and file. Four vice presidents got the axe from ChannelAdvisor, which laid off a total of 70 people after raising a new financing round of $20 million. CEO Scot Wingo confirmed Internet reports that the affected veeps were Kevin Fitzgerald, head of U.S. sales; Jennifer Gibson, head of international sales; Ralph Kasuba, head of engineering; and Walter Goodlett, head of human resources.


October 2008 - 45 employees cut from ChannelAdvisor

ChannelAdvisor, which sells software that retailers use to sell and market goods over the Internet, cut about 45 employees in Research Triangle Park.


January 2009 - ChannelAdvisor Lays off further 19% of Workforce

In September, the company had laid off 20 percent of its 350 employees. On Thursday, ChannelAdvisor laid off another 19 percent, a 35 percent reduction in total from its pre-September level. This week's cuts were made in departments across-the-board, with half occurring in the US and half in the UK and Australian offices. Even with an additional $20 million in funding raised in September, Wingo said the second round of layoffs were necessary because "2009 is very hard to figure out what's going to happen." Customers were not notified of the layoffs ahead of time. Would they feel the effects of a 35 percent reduction in staff in 4 months?


October 2008 - Infopia Announces Layoffs

Infopia laid off 12 people on Monday, leaving them with 50 employees. President and CEO Bjorn Espenes said the move came at a time when, due to the economy, "everyone is being a little cautious." eBay had also announced layoffs on Monday impacting 10 percent of its workforce.


January 2009 - Infopia CEO & Co founder Bjorn Espenes replaced

Infopia announced that Coleman Barney has joined the company as President and Chief Executive Officer, succeeding Bjorn Espenes, who will continue as a member of the Board of Directors for Infopia.
