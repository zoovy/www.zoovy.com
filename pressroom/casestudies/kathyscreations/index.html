Case Studies

Kathy Kilna's Creations
 Kathy Kilna had a dream - to one day leave her job as a service representative for an insurance company and sell her hand-made jewelry full time. With a build-it-yourself e-commerce storefront from Zoovy, she tripled her sales in just a few months and fulfilled her dream of becoming a full time jewelry designer.

Ever since she was a child, Kathy had been intrigued by jewelry and displayed a natural ability for jewelry repair. Over the years, she accumulated so many of her creations that she was unable to wear them all. Hoping to support her hobby, she began selling her jewelry and established her business, Kathy Kilna's Creations.

Working at craft fairs on weekends, Kathy developed a loyal following for her jewelry products. She soon supplemented that effort by selling products on Internet auction sites. Although she had some success selling her products on eBay, she found the process tedious and time consuming.

"Most of the bidding on eBay takes place on the weekends," said Kathy. "I spent so much time online setting up the bids, processing orders, getting the closing price, collecting the email addresses of buyers and trying to manage the process, it was tiring. And it left little time to create my jewelry."

Kathy decided to look for a way to set up an online web store for Kilna Creations where she had more control over the process and her time. Searching the Internet, she found that Zoovy, Inc. offered a 30-day free trial for its build-it-yourself e-commerce storefront called Zoovy Merchant.

Zoovy offers affordable, easy-to-use e-commerce services designed for the way successful small businesses operate. The company's build-it-yourself program features a user-friendly, step-by-step store-building interface that smoothly guides business owners through the creation of their Web store. Zoovy incorporates unique features like auction integration, convenient Windows applications, content management and customer relationship tools that maximize sales for small business owners - all supported by personal customer service.

"I had a basic storefront set up in 15 minutes, then quickly added photos of my products," said Kathy. "The service makes tailoring any page of your site a breeze, and it offers a wide range of customization options."

Zoovy provides customers with a Windows-based program called Product Manager that allows merchants to set-up and update their web store offline. Using Product Manager, Kathy is able to set-up online auctions for future dates and times. Once uploaded to her web storefront, Zoovy automatically runs the auctions at the pre-set times and brings the winning bidder back to Kathy's storefront for check out. Kathy can then offer impulse items to entice the bidder to buy more.

"A recent winning bidder purchased some jewelry after seeing it on the shopping cart," said Kathy. "And Zoovy automatically sends out follow-up emails so I can sell more products to existing customers."

Zoovy web stores have automated email tools that send auto responses to customers when orders are received, confirmed and shipped. The simple tools also personalize newsletters with the customer's name and allow merchants to target selected customers for email specials and sales. For example, a merchant can send a special offer to customers who have not purchased in the last 60 days or to customers who have spent a certain amount on their website.

While selling at local craft fairs, Kathy found that special orders were always a common request. Customers had many different preferences regarding jewelry, necklace length, crystal color, bead color, earring style, and more. With Zoovy's customization options, Kathy was able to set up drop down menus and color charts so customers can select the jewelry to suit their needs.

"One of my most popular items is a Victorian Cascade. Customers can choose the bead color using a color chart on my web store," continued Kathy. "Customized products have become a unique selling feature for my business."

Kathy also liked the variety of payment and delivery options provided by Zoovy. Since she already had an account with Iongate for processing credit cards, Zoovy accommodated that in her new web store. She could also offer customers alternative payment methods, from cash, checks and credit cards to micro payments such as PayPal and MoneyZap. Delivery options are similarly flexible, with the added ability to automatically calculate cost based on weight and add handling fees or insurance.

According to Kathy Kilna, the true benefit of using Zoovy is the flexibility, control and added time she now has for her passion for jewelry design. She estimates that in three or four months she will be making as much or more in income than she was in her insurance job.

"Zoovy has empowered me to take control of my life," said Kathy. "I am looking forward to working at home and reaching more people so that they can experience a whole new way to buy jewelry."
